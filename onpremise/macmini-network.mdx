Update the network configuration of a Mac mini robot after moving the device or bringing it back into service: IP address, gateway, and whatever else a stable connection to Kapptivate requires.

## Prerequisites

- An external display (HDMI or USB-C)
- A keyboard and mouse (wired or Bluetooth)
- An RJ45 cable connected to your network, with the necessary flows open toward Kapptivate
- The IP address, subnet mask, and gateway from your IT team

## Configuration

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the network settings">
    Turn on the Mac mini and go to **(Apple) > System Settings > Network**.

    <Frame caption="macOS network settings">
      ![Network settings](/images/onpremise/macmini-network-01.webp)
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure the Ethernet interface">
    Select the Ethernet interface and click **Advanced**.

    <Frame caption="The Ethernet interface">
      ![Ethernet interface](/images/onpremise/macmini-network-02.webp)
    </Frame>

    In the **TCP/IP** tab, choose **Configure IPv4 manually** and fill in the IP address, subnet mask, and gateway. Click **OK**, then **Apply**.

    <Frame caption="Manual IPv4 configuration">
      ![Manual IPv4](/images/onpremise/macmini-network-03.webp)
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify connectivity">
    Open Terminal and check both flows toward the platform:

    ```bash
    curl -v telnet://app.kapptivate.com:443
    curl -v telnet://app.kapptivate.com:5671
    ```

    Replace `app` with your own domain if needed (you can read it in your platform URL: `your_domain.kapptivate.com`).
  </Step>
</Steps>

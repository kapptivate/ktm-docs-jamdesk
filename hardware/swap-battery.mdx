---
title: Swap the battery of a robot
description: Replace the motherboard battery on new and old robot hardware.
---

Every robot ships with a motherboard battery. When it runs out, the robot can malfunction (clock drift, BIOS resets); replacing it takes a few minutes. The procedure depends on your hardware generation.

## New robot hardware

New hardware is gray or green:

<Frame caption="New generation hardware">
  ![New hardware](/images/hardware/swap-battery-01.webp)
</Frame>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the robot">
    Remove the 2 screws and take off the top aluminum plate.

    <Frame caption="The 2 screws to remove">
      ![Screws to remove](/images/hardware/swap-battery-02.webp)
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Locate the battery">
    The battery sits on the motherboard, next to the power supply.

    <Frame caption="The battery next to the power supply">
      ![Battery location](/images/hardware/swap-battery-03.webp)
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Replace it">
    Detach the adhesive securing the battery with a thin tool, pull gently on the red and black cable to remove it, and stick the new battery in the same spot.

    <Frame caption="Detach the adhesive, then swap the battery">
      ![Detach the adhesive](/images/hardware/swap-battery-04.webp)
      ![Insert the new battery](/images/hardware/swap-battery-05.webp)
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Close and update the BIOS">
    Screw the top plate back in place. Then update the BIOS before using the robot again; contact support for the BIOS update guide for your model.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Old robot hardware

Older hardware looks like this:

<Frame caption="Old generation hardware">
  ![Old hardware](/images/hardware/swap-battery-06.webp)
</Frame>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the robot">
    Remove the 2 screws and slide the top aluminum plate towards the back.

    <Frame caption="The 2 screws to remove">
      ![Screws to remove](/images/hardware/swap-battery-07.webp)
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Locate the battery">
    The battery is in the lower left corner of the motherboard.

    <Frame caption="Battery in the lower left corner">
      ![Battery location](/images/hardware/swap-battery-08.webp)
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Replace it">
    Detach the adhesive with a thin tool, pull gently on the red and black cable, and stick the new battery in the same spot.

    <Frame caption="Detach the adhesive, then swap the battery">
      ![Detach the adhesive](/images/hardware/swap-battery-09.webp)
      ![Insert the new battery](/images/hardware/swap-battery-10.webp)
    </Frame>

    If access is difficult, unscrew the four screws at the corners of the motherboard and lift it, without disconnecting anything, to reach the battery. Reattach those screws before closing.

    <Frame caption="Lift the motherboard for easier access">
      ![Motherboard screws](/images/hardware/swap-battery-11.webp)
      ![Lifted motherboard](/images/hardware/swap-battery-12.webp)
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Close and update the BIOS">
    Screw the top plate back. Update the BIOS before using the robot again; contact support for the guide.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Smartphone batteries swelling

Smartphone batteries swell over time when constantly charging, which is the case on a smartphone robot. A stand reduces heat and delays the phenomenon, but the real fix is choosing phones that support **pass-through charging**: power feeds the phone directly without cycling the battery.

Models known to support it: Galaxy S22 and S23 series, Galaxy Z Fold 2 through 5, Galaxy Z Flip 5, Galaxy A73 5G, and Galaxy A33 5G (the most affordable).
